Service

Service in Bryan-College Station

In my time at Texas A&M, I have participated in many service events around the Bryan-College Station area. I volunteered with The Big Event where I went to houses in the community and helped them with projects and tasks in and around their property. Kyle Field Day is another event that I have participated in these past 2 years. My student organization, PREP, had a booth where we decorated brown lunch bags with motivational and fun messages. These bags were delivered to kids in the area who are on meal plans so that they get a positive message every day with their lunch at school. Within PREP, I went to weekly park cleanups hosted by the Keep Brazos Beautiful organization to help pick up trash at parks in the area. One of the biggest events that I take part in is Shack-a-thon, an event put on by Aggie Habitat for Humanity to raise awareness and funds about homelessness. As the Service Executive for PREP last year, it was my job to ensure that PREP made a Shack, and participated in the events that helped raise money for Aggie Habitat for Humanity!

Service in Austin

Before I had the opportunity to come serve on and around campus in College Station, I was active within the Austin area. In high school, I served as the President of my National Honor Society. As a member and leader of NHS, I helped coordinate our school's involvement in The Linus Project, a non-profit organization that makes homemade blankets for children in need. We helped them by creating tags and ribbons for the blankets as well as preparing the material that would be used for the blankets. NHS also helped put on the Eggstravaganza event at a local park. At this event, we would help hide Easter Eggs for kids to find during their hunt. Before and after the hunt, we would be working the booths and tables that had fun activities for the kids to part take in. Outside of NHS, at the end of every summer, I went to help Hindu Charities 4 America, HC4A, pack school supplies for children of low-income families.
